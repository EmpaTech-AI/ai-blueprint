## Section 2: Strategic Context

**top_priorities:**
1. Grow permanent placement revenue by 25% over the next 12 months — we are currently at approximately €4.8M total revenue and want to reach €6M primarily through higher-margin executive search and tech sector mandates, without proportional headcount growth in delivery.
2. Reduce time-to-fill on contingency search mandates from our current average of 38 days to under 28 days. Clients are increasingly asking us to compete on speed, and two of our largest clients have specifically cited turnaround time as the reason they dual-source with competitors.
3. Launch a formal RPO (Recruitment Process Outsourcing) service line by Q4 2026 — we have been doing informal embedded recruitment for two clients but have no structured product, SLA framework, or pricing model. We see RPO as our highest-margin growth opportunity.

**competitive_threat:**
Our biggest competitive threat is from two directions simultaneously. First, large international staffing firms (Manpower, Adecco, Hays) are increasing their CEE presence and undercutting on price for volume roles, which squeezes our contingency business. Second, in-house talent acquisition teams at mid-market tech companies are increasingly capable — LinkedIn Recruiter, Greenhouse, and internal sourcers are taking roles we would have filled 3 years ago. We are not competing on volume or price; we need to compete on quality, speed, and specialist knowledge. We are not doing this consistently enough today.

**growth_targets:**
Revenue: €4.8M → €6M by end of FY2026 (25% growth). Executive search mix: currently 18% of revenue, target 30% by end of FY2026. Headcount: 68 employees currently, plan to add 6–8 delivery staff (researchers/resourcers) in H2 2026 if the RPO product launches successfully. No plans to open new geographic offices in 2026 — growth to come from existing markets and remote delivery.

**active_initiatives:**
1. We are in the process of migrating from our current ATS (Zoho Recruit) to Vincere (a specialist recruitment CRM/ATS). Migration was started in January 2026, currently approximately 40% complete. We expect full cutover by end of July 2026. This is causing some disruption — consultants are working across two systems simultaneously.
2. We ran an internal process audit in Q4 2025 covering our search methodology. The output was a new 7-stage search framework (Define > Source > Screen > Shortlist > Submit > Interview Support > Close) that we are now trying to standardise across teams. Adoption is inconsistent — senior consultants largely ignore it.
3. Early conversations with one of our RPO pilot clients (a 400-person Warsaw-based fintech) about formalising the arrangement and potentially expanding scope.

## Section 3: Operational Pain Points

**pain_point_1:**
Candidate sourcing and pipeline building is massively manual and time-inefficient. A researcher on a typical mid-level technology mandate spends 6–8 hours on initial sourcing: searching LinkedIn Recruiter, Boolean searches across job boards, manually reviewing profiles, and building a longlist of 40–60 candidates before any contact is made. We run approximately 35–40 active mandates per month. This bottleneck is the single biggest reason our time-to-fill is 38 days — the sourcing phase alone takes 5–7 days on average. We have tried LinkedIn Recruiter's AI suggestions feature but found it consistently surfaces irrelevant profiles. We do not have a tool that learns from our past successful placements to suggest similar candidates.

**pain_point_2:**
Our candidate database (currently in Zoho Recruit, migrating to Vincere) is effectively dead weight. We have approximately 47,000 candidate records accumulated over 9 years, but nobody uses them for active sourcing. The data quality is poor — probably 30–40% of records have outdated contact details, missing skills data, or duplicate entries. Consultants default to starting fresh on LinkedIn for every new mandate rather than mining the existing database because "the database takes longer than just searching LinkedIn fresh." We are paying for a database that provides zero sourcing value. We have not invested in data cleaning or enrichment.

**pain_point_3:**
Client reporting and activity updates are entirely manual and inconsistently delivered. Each consultant is responsible for their own client communication, and the format, frequency, and quality varies enormously. Some consultants send weekly update emails; others go quiet for two weeks and only contact the client when they have something to submit. We have no standard reporting template, no CRM-driven reminders, and no dashboard that clients can log into. In our Q1 2026 client satisfaction survey (12 clients responded), "communication and transparency" was rated 3.1 out of 5 — our lowest-rated dimension. Two clients mentioned lack of visibility into search progress as a source of frustration.

**pain_point_4:**
CV formatting and submission is a manual process that consumes approximately 1.5–2 hours per candidate submission. Our standard process requires a consultant to reformat the candidate's original CV to our branded template, write a 150–200 word "candidate summary" paragraph, and compile a covering note explaining why this candidate fits the brief. On a typical shortlist of 5 candidates, this is 8–10 hours of work per mandate, done by the consultant themselves (not support staff). This is high-value consultant time spent on low-value formatting work. We have no template automation, no AI writing assistance, and submissions are done manually in Word.

**pain_point_5:**
Interview and offer management process is fragmented and relies on email and calendar coordination that frequently breaks down. Scheduling a 4-person interview panel for a senior role can take 3–5 email exchanges over 2–3 days. We have no scheduling tool. Offer management is tracked in a personal spreadsheet maintained by each consultant — there is no shared pipeline view. The Managing Director has no real-time visibility into where each mandate stands without asking consultants individually.

**tried_solutions:**
For sourcing (Pain Point 1): We tried LinkedIn Recruiter's AI recommendation feature — it did not surface relevant candidates for specialist roles. We also briefly trialled an external sourcing tool (Lusha) for contact enrichment but stopped using it after 3 months because the data quality for Eastern European candidates was poor.
For the candidate database (Pain Point 2): We commissioned a data cleaning exercise in 2023 using a freelancer — approximately 8,000 records were cleaned before the budget ran out. The problem immediately recurred because there was no ongoing governance process to prevent re-contamination.
For client reporting (Pain Point 3): We attempted to standardise reporting in 2024 by creating a Word template. Adoption lasted approximately 6 weeks before consultants reverted to ad hoc emails.
For CV formatting (Pain Point 4): We explored hiring a VA (virtual assistant) to handle formatting in 2025. One VA was engaged for 3 months. The quality was inconsistent and the communication overhead with the VA consumed much of the time saved.

## Section 5: People & Culture

**leadership_ai_attitude:**
Open — interested but cautious

**ai_training_done:**
No formal AI training has been done. The CEO (Dimitar Popov) attended a half-day AI for Business workshop in October 2025 as part of a CEE business leaders programme. He came back enthusiastic about AI for productivity and has been informally encouraging consultants to "explore AI tools." However, there has been no structured programme, no shared learnings, and no budget allocated for training. A few consultants have self-taught ChatGPT use for specific tasks.

**internal_champion:**
Operations Director (currently acting as both Ops Director and interim IT lead). She has been driving the Vincere migration and has expressed interest in evaluating AI tools for recruitment workflow automation. She has the ear of the CEO and sufficient technical literacy to evaluate tools independently.

**resistance_points:**
1. Consultant autonomy culture: Senior consultants (particularly the two Partners who manage the executive search practice) view their sourcing methodology as proprietary. Any suggestion that AI could replicate or augment their search process is met with scepticism — they see it as undermining their expertise and, potentially, their fee justification.
2. Data quality anxiety: Several consultants are reluctant to engage with the Vincere migration because they know the underlying data is poor. There is a "why bother if the data is bad" attitude that extends to any AI tools that rely on internal data.
3. Billing model concern: Two consultants raised, in an internal meeting, the concern that if AI makes recruitment faster and easier, it could be used to justify lowering fees. This is a legitimate cultural concern that has not been addressed by leadership.

## Section 6: Data Readiness

**clean_data:**
Partially — some areas are clean, others aren't

**valuable_data_location:**
Our most valuable data lives in three places: (1) Vincere/Zoho Recruit — candidate profiles, placement history, client accounts, and mandate records. Currently partially migrated, data quality mixed as described above. (2) Xero — financial records including revenue per client, invoice history, and payment terms. This data is clean and reliable but has never been used analytically. (3) LinkedIn Recruiter — sourcing activity and candidate engagement data, but this is locked inside LinkedIn and not exportable in any meaningful structured form. Consultant-specific knowledge (who they know, which candidates are "warm") is almost entirely in individual email inboxes and personal notes — not in any system.

**data_governance:**
Informal — some ownership but no formal process

**compliance_constraints:**
GDPR is our primary compliance constraint and it is significant. We hold personal data on approximately 47,000 candidates across Bulgaria, Romania, Poland, and other EU jurisdictions. We have a basic privacy policy and GDPR registration in Bulgaria, but our data governance is immature — we do not have documented data retention policies, no formal process for handling Right to Access or Right to Erasure requests (we have received 3 such requests in the past year and handled them manually and inconsistently), and our consent records for candidates added before 2021 are incomplete. Any AI tool we implement that touches candidate data must be EU-hosted or at minimum GDPR-compliant, and we need to be careful about using candidate data to train external AI models.
