# The AI Value Blueprint — Product Overview

*A guide for new team members and business stakeholders. No technical background needed — by the end you'll understand what this product is, why we built it, who it's for, and how it works.*

---

## 1. In one paragraph

The **AI Value Blueprint** is a productized AI diagnostic from **AI Assist BG**. A company gives us their documents and answers a short intake form; we give them back a polished 12–18 page report that tells them, specifically and with evidence: *where AI can create the most value in their business, how ready they are to capture it, and the order in which they should pursue it.* It's a fast, affordable, standalone product — and it's also the front door to our deeper consulting work.

---

## 2. Why this product exists

Most companies today know AI matters, but they're stuck on three questions:

1. **"Where do we even start?"** — they're surrounded by hype and don't know which opportunities are real for *their* business.
2. **"Are we even ready?"** — they don't know whether their data, systems, people, and governance can support AI yet.
3. **"What's it going to cost us to find out?"** — a full consulting audit is a large commitment, and they're not ready to make it blind.

Our flagship engagement, the **full AI Company Audit**, answers all of this in depth — but it's a significant investment (roughly €75K+) and a big first step for a company that's still unsure.

The **AI Value Blueprint** solves that. It's a **compressed, lower-cost version of the same methodology** (roughly €7.5K–€10K) that delivers a real, evidence-based diagnostic in a fraction of the time. For the client, it's a low-risk way to get genuine clarity. For us, it does three things at once:

- **Delivers standalone value** — the client gets something genuinely useful even if they never buy anything else.
- **Builds trust** — they experience the quality of our thinking before committing to a large engagement.
- **Creates a natural bridge** — the Blueprint ends by showing what a full audit would add, turning satisfied Blueprint clients into audit clients.

In short: it widens the top of our funnel, productizes our expertise so we can serve more clients, and lets prospects "try before they buy."

---

## 3. Who it's for, and who's involved

**The client** is a small-to-mid-sized company that wants a credible, no-nonsense read on its AI opportunities. They contribute their materials (documents + a form) and, at the end, receive the finished Blueprint.

**The AI Assist consultant/reviewer** runs the engagement, reviews the AI-generated analysis, and approves it before it ever reaches the client. **A human is always in the loop** — the system never sends anything to a client on its own.

**The pipeline** (our AI-powered engine) does the heavy analytical lifting — reading the materials, scoring, generating opportunities, sequencing them, and assembling the document — so the consultant's time goes into judgment and review, not grunt work.

---

## 4. What the client actually receives

A single, professionally formatted report (delivered as PDF, Word, and web versions) in AI Assist's house style. It contains:

- **Executive Summary** — the core message on one page, readable by a busy CEO.
- **AI Readiness Snapshot** — how the company scores across six dimensions of readiness.
- **Key Findings** — where value is currently being lost, backed by their own evidence.
- **AI Opportunity Map** — the top 5–7 AI opportunities, scored and explained in plain business terms.
- **Recommended Action Sequence** — what to do *now*, *next*, and *later*.
- **Readiness Gaps & Recommendations** — what they need to fix to unlock the bigger opportunities.
- **Next Steps** — concrete actions, including where deeper help would pay off.

The goal: a client should finish reading and think *"they clearly understood our business, and now we know exactly what to do."*

---

## 5. How it works — the five-stage journey

Think of it as an assembly line. The client's raw materials go in one end; a finished, polished report comes out the other. Each stage does one job and hands its work to the next.

**Stage 1 — Intake.** We take everything the client gave us (documents, form answers) and distill it into a clean internal summary: who they are, what hurts (their "pain points"), and a set of hypotheses about where AI could help.

**Stage 2 — Maturity.** We assess how ready the company is across **six dimensions** — *Strategy, Data, Technology, People, Processes, Governance* — each rated **Early, Developing, or Established**. This tells us not just what's possible, but what's *realistic* for them right now.

**Stage 3 — Opportunities.** We identify and score the top **5–7 AI opportunities**. Each is rated on **Impact, Feasibility, and Alignment** with their goals, then sorted into three buckets:
- **Quick Wins** — high feasibility, start immediately.
- **Foundation Builders** — things that unlock future opportunities.
- **Big Bets** — high impact but need investment or more maturity first.

Crucially, readiness limits feasibility: a brilliant idea that depends on clean data won't score as "easy" if the company's data isn't ready. The diagnosis stays honest.

**Stage 4 — Roadmap.** We sequence the opportunities into **Now (months 1–3), Next (3–6), and Later (6–12)** — balancing early wins (to build momentum) with the foundational work that has to come first.

**Stage 5 — Assembly.** We compile all of the above into the single, polished, client-facing document, written for an executive audience and formatted in our house style.

## 6. What makes it trustworthy

This is the part that sets the Blueprint apart from "ask a chatbot what AI we should do." Three disciplines make the output defensible:

**Everything is grounded in evidence.** As the analysis is built, every factual claim is internally tagged by how well it's supported — is it backed by a document the client gave us, by something they stated on the form, or is it an informed inference? We track the ratio, so a reviewer can instantly see how much of a Blueprint rests on hard evidence versus professional judgment. Low-confidence items are surfaced for the consultant to verify — they don't quietly become "facts."

**The same inputs always produce the same output.** This sounds obvious but is genuinely hard with AI, and it's one of the biggest things we've engineered for. A consulting product can't give a client one set of scores on Monday and different ones on Tuesday — that would destroy trust and defensibility. So the system is built so that identical inputs yield an identical Blueprint: the same opportunities, the same scores, the same roadmap, every time. (A large share of our ongoing engineering work goes into protecting this property.)

**A human approves everything, and quality gates catch problems first.** Before a Blueprint can be released, automated checks scan it for issues — formatting leaks, internal notes that shouldn't be client-facing, inconsistencies, a wrong name, and so on. Serious issues are flagged as **blockers**, and a blocked Blueprint simply *cannot be sent to the client* until a reviewer resolves it. The consultant then reviews and explicitly approves. Nothing reaches a client by accident.

## 7. The tools behind it (a light tour)

You don't need to know the code, but it helps to know the pieces:

- **The Client Portal** — where clients upload their materials and, once approved, download their finished Blueprint.
- **The Admin Console** — where AI Assist staff run engagements, watch progress through the five stages, see the evidence/confidence scores for each stage, review reviewer flags, and approve the final document.
- **The Document Lab** — a preview tool that lets the team see exactly how a finished document will look (formatting, layout, branding) without re-running a whole engagement — so we can perfect the presentation quickly.
- **Reviewer metadata** — a behind-the-scenes record attached to every run (which version of the system produced it, the confidence scores per stage, any flags) so every Blueprint is traceable and auditable.

---

## 9. What the Blueprint deliberately does *not* do

Being clear about scope is part of being trustworthy. The Blueprint is a **directional diagnostic**, not a full execution plan. It intentionally leaves the following to the full AI Company Audit:

- Detailed implementation roadmaps with milestones, dependencies, and capacity planning
- Financial ROI models, NPV, and scenario analysis
- A full AI governance framework with risk tiering and policies
- Stakeholder interviews and deep process mapping
- Custom financial modeling tied to the client's P&L

This keeps the Blueprint fast and affordable, and gives the full audit a clear, valuable reason to exist.

## 10. Mini-glossary (the vocabulary you'll hear)

- **Pipeline** — the five-stage assembly line that turns client materials into a finished Blueprint.
- **The six dimensions** — Strategy, Data, Technology, People, Processes, Governance — the axes of AI readiness.
- **Maturity levels** — Early / Developing / Established — how far along a company is on each dimension.
- **Quick Win / Foundation Builder / Big Bet** — the three categories every opportunity is sorted into.
- **Now / Next / Later** — the three time horizons of the roadmap.
- **Grounding / confidence** — how well-supported a claim is by the client's actual evidence.
- **Reproducibility** — the guarantee that identical inputs always produce an identical Blueprint.
- **Reviewer flag / blocker** — an automated warning; a *blocker* prevents release until it's resolved.
- **Human-in-the-loop** — the principle that a consultant always reviews and approves before a client sees anything.
