## A) Executive Summary of Current State

Meridian Talent Partners OOD is a Sofia-headquartered B2B recruitment and talent solutions firm employing 68 FTEs across three offices in Bulgaria, Romania, and Poland [Document-Backed — org chart p.1]. The firm operates four service lines (permanent placement, executive search, contract staffing, RPO) across three primary verticals (Technology & Engineering, Finance & Legal, Manufacturing & Supply Chain) and generated EUR 4,823,400 in FY2025 revenue at 71.3% gross margin and 8.7% EBITDA margin [Document-Backed — financial summary p.1-2].

The FY2026 strategic plan is built on four board-approved priorities: Speed (reduce average time-to-fill from 38 to 28 days), Scale (formalise the RPO service line targeting EUR 600,000+ revenue), Smarter Delivery (reduce consultant admin burden by 30% and lift client satisfaction from 3.83 to 4.20 out of 5.0), and Executive Search Growth (lift exec search from 18% to 30% of revenue) [Document-Backed — strategic plan p.1-2; form: strategic context]. The plan explicitly targets EUR 6,000,000 total revenue at 25% growth [Document-Backed — strategic plan p.2].

The most urgent operational constraint is candidate sourcing: the sourcing phase accounts for 22 of the 38-day average time-to-fill (58% of total TTF), driven by 6–8 hours of manual researcher effort per mandate across 35–40 active mandates monthly [Document-Backed — sales pipeline p.3; SOP p.2]. The 47,000-record candidate database provides effectively zero sourcing value due to ~35% stale data, and consultants spend 1.5–2 hours per candidate on CV reformatting consuming 8–10 hours per mandate at the consultant tier [Document-Backed — tech inventory p.2; SOP p.3].

Meridian is at a defined inflection point: the Vincere ATS migration (40% complete, targeting July 2026 cutover) creates a narrow window to establish data governance before the new system goes live [Document-Backed — tech inventory p.1; strategic plan p.1]. Operations Director Mihailova is the internal AI champion with both technical literacy and CEO access [Document-Backed — org chart p.2]; the two Senior Partners (Ivanov in Tech & Engineering, Georgieva in Finance & Legal) are the primary cultural resistance points [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.4; form: people & culture].

## C) Detected Pain Points (Prioritized)

### Pain Point 1 — Manual Candidate Sourcing Bottleneck: Primary TTF Driver

**Statement:** Candidate sourcing is entirely manual, consuming 6–8 researcher hours per mandate and accounting for 58% of total time-to-fill. At 35–40 active mandates per month, this is the single largest operational constraint on revenue growth and the primary obstacle to the 28-day TTF target.

**Evidence:**
- Sourcing phase averages 22 of 38 total days-to-fill; submission-to-offer phase is broadly on target [Document-Backed — sales pipeline p.3]
- SOP Process 2 documents 3–4 hours for LinkedIn Boolean search + 1–2 hours for job board search + 45 min for longlist review, totalling 5–6.75 hours minimum per mandate [Document-Backed — SOP p.2]
- LinkedIn Recruiter AI suggestions rated unreliable for specialist roles; Lusha trial abandoned for CEE data quality [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.1-2]
- Candidate database provides near-zero sourcing value; researchers spend under 15 minutes on database search per mandate [Document-Backed — SOP p.2; tech inventory p.2]

**Impact area:** Revenue / Time / Cost
**Severity:** High
**Confidence:** [Document-Backed] — corroborated across financial summary, sales pipeline, SOP, tech inventory, and previous AI initiatives

### Pain Point 2 — Unusable Candidate Database: EUR 9,600/Year Asset Providing Zero Value

**Statement:** 47,000 candidate records accumulated over 9 years are effectively unused for active sourcing. Poor data quality, absent governance, and no AI-readiness mean the database is a liability rather than a competitive asset, and the Vincere migration is proceeding without a systematic cleansing plan.

**Evidence:**
- Tech inventory rates candidate profile data as POOR quality; AI readiness requires "data cleaning and GDPR consent audit first" [Document-Backed — tech inventory p.2]
- 2023 cleaning project cleaned ~8,000 of 47,000 records before budget ran out; no governance process established; data degraded immediately [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.2]
- SOP documents researchers spending <15 minutes on database search per mandate, effectively skipping it [Document-Backed — SOP p.2]
- Pre-2021 consent records incomplete; GDPR Right to Erasure requests handled inconsistently [Document-Backed — tech inventory p.2; form: data readiness]
- Zoho Recruit licence (EUR 9,600/year) being paid for a system providing no sourcing value [Document-Backed — tech inventory p.1]

**Impact area:** Cost / Time / Strategic
**Severity:** High
**Confidence:** [Document-Backed] — corroborated across tech inventory, previous AI initiatives, SOP, and form responses

Consultants spend 95–125 minutes per candidate on CV reformatting, summary writing, and submission pack compilation [Document-Backed — SOP p.3]. An AI writing tool with a standardised prompt library and Meridian's branded template could reduce this to under 30 minutes per candidate, the target stated in the FY2026 strategic plan [Document-Backed — strategic plan p.1]. At 5 candidates per shortlist and 35–40 mandates per month, this represents approximately 200–300 hours of consultant time per month that could be redirected to business development or client management [Inferred — derivation per appendix item 1].

**Supporting evidence:**
- VA trial failed due to quality inconsistency — AI is the correct solution for variable formatting tasks [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.3]
- 6–8 consultants already saving ~20 min per summary with informal ChatGPT use [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.4]
- Strategic plan targets ≤30 min per candidate as FY2026 success metric [Document-Backed — strategic plan p.1]
- A company ChatGPT account with shared prompt library would resolve the live GDPR risk in parallel [Document-Backed — previous AI initiatives p.4]

**What we'd validate next:** Audit the current Meridian CV template and candidate summary format to build a standardised prompt library; confirm a company OpenAI or Microsoft Copilot account satisfies EU data processing requirements.

**Classification hypothesis:** Quick Win
**Linked Pain Point(s):** PP3, PP5
